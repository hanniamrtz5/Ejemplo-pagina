In The Lottery by Shirley Jackson, there is no literal "letter" written by the lady, but if we interpret "the letter of the lady" symbolically, it could represent the role of Mrs. Hutchinson as a figure of protest against the town's brutal tradition. Her desperate plea for fairness when she is chosen in the lottery can be seen as her 'letter' of defiance, questioning the system that unquestioningly sacrifices an individual for the sake of tradition.
